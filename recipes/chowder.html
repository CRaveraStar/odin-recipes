New England Clam Chowder Recipe

Ingredients

  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon, diced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 ribs celery, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups clam juice (from bottled or reserved from clams)
  • 1½ pounds Russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves (or ½ teaspoon dried)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 (6.5-ounce) cans chopped clams, drained and juice reserved
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)

Equipment

  • Large soup pot or Dutch oven
  • Wooden spoon
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Peeler and knife for potatoes
  • Ladle for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crisp. Remove bacon and drain on paper towels. Leave about 2 tablespoons of bacon fat in the pot.
  2. Add the butter, onion, celery, and garlic. Cook until the onions are translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle in the flour and stir for 1 minute to form a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in the clam juice, scraping the bottom of the pot. Add potatoes, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes until potatoes are tender.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and clams. Simmer gently for 5 minutes — do not let it boil or the cream may curdle.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove bay leaves, return bacon to the pot, and stir.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley or oyster crackers.
