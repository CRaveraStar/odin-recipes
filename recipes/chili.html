Texas Chili Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 (14.5-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (12-ounce) bottle dark beer or beef broth
  • 2 cups beef stock
  • 1 tablespoon masa harina or cornmeal (optional, for thickening)

Equipment

  • Large Dutch oven or heavy pot
  • Wooden spoon
  • Chef’s knife
  • Measuring spoons and cups
  • Ladle for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes in batches and sear until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook another minute.
  3. Add chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Stir to coat the onions and garlic in the spices.
  4. Return the browned beef to the pot. Add crushed tomatoes, beer (or broth), and beef stock. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  5. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer gently for 1½ to 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the beef is tender and the flavors meld.
  6. For a thicker chili, stir in masa harina or cornmeal and simmer uncovered for 10 more minutes.
  7. Adjust seasoning to taste. Serve hot with diced onions, shredded cheese, or jalapeños as desired.
